This is the tools page

Crocheting demands just a handful of essential tools: a crochet hook, yarn, scissors, and a tapestry needle. These are easy to find and affordable, making crocheting accessible to all. Optional accessories like stitch markers and row counters can enhance the experience, though they're not necessary for beginners. With these basic tools at hand, anyone can embark on a journey of creativity and craft, transforming yarn into beautiful works of art.

What You'll Need

Yarn

Yarn is one of the most necessary things needed to make works of crochet because it is the material you work with to get the final product. Yarn comes in different “yarn weights”. This is important for picking your hook size.

Crochet Hook

The hook is the actual tool used to make the works. These hooks range in size starting as small as 2.75mm and getting as large as 25mm. The size of your hook is dependent on the type and size of the yarn you pick. This information can be found on the paper holding the yarn skein together.

Scissors

Scissors will be used to cut the yarn. You may work on projects that require changing colors. You’ll need scissors to cut the yarn you were working with as well as at the end of the project.

Tape Measure

This will help when making some patterns, especially clothes as you can get accurate measurements and ensure whatever you're making will reach the requirements and fit properly.

Stitch Marker

Stitch markers will help you keep track of where you are in your work. They are especially helpful when working in the round to mark the start of the row.

Yarn Needle

The yarn needle will be beneficial for weaving in the ends of your work and attaching additional pieces if required.

Stitch/Row Counter

A stitch or row counter will be beneficial when doing long repetitive works. They will help you keep track of how many stitches or rows you've done so far. There are many apps that will do this.

Blocking Mat

Blocking mats are beneficial to help end your projects. The way yar gets worked it doesn't always come out in the shape you intend and may curl. The blocking mat is a surface you can pin your work to to force it into the shape you intended.

Storage

Although a storage container is not necessary it is incredibly beneficial to have all your crochet tools in the same place.
